ASSIGNMENT 4: TEXT ANALYTICS USING QUANTEDA
1. Introduction
This assignment applies the quanteda package in R to perform text analytics on US presidential inaugural speeches and Department of Defense (DOD) reports. The workflow includes corpus creation, tokenization, construction of document-feature matrices (DFM), and scaling methods such as Wordfish to estimate document positions along latent rhetorical dimensions.
2. Analysis of US Presidential Inaugural Speeches
2.1 Data and Method
The built-in data_corpus_inaugural from quanteda was used, subset to include speeches from 2000-2025 (7 speeches). Texts were preprocessed by:
· Removing punctuation, numbers, symbols, and URLs
· Converting to lowercase
· Removing English stopwords and common functional words (will, can, must, shall, may, one, two, three)
A document-feature matrix (DFM) was constructed and trimmed to retain words appearing in at least 2 documents and no more than 80% of documents.

3. What is Wordfish?
Wordfish is an unsupervised text scaling method that estimates the position of documents along a latent dimension using word frequencies.
Key Features:
· Does not require labeled data
· Identifies hidden patterns in text
· Assigns each document a numerical position (theta)
· Helps compare documents based on language use
· Widely used in political text analysis to identify differences in rhetoric and positioning
How it works:
Wordfish assumes a Poisson distribution for word counts and estimates:
· Document positions (theta) - where each document falls on the latent dimension
· Feature parameters (beta) - how strongly each word discriminates
· Feature fixed effects (psi) - word frequency baselines
