Cross-Model Prompting Workflow for a Systematic Literature Review

1. Initial Prompt Creation

Baseline Prompt

Conduct a 2,000-word structured systematic literature review on the applications of
data mining and machine learning in real-world domains. Include a methodology
section, synthesize key findings, identify major research trends and gaps, and
propose one testable hypothesis. Use an academic tone and follow systematic
literature review standards.

3. Refine the Prompt

Refined Prompt for ChatGPT

Requires numerical screening details, formal quality appraisal, and academic referencing while preserving analytical synthesis and cautious hypothesis formulation.

Refined Prompt for Copilot

Builds on strong methodological rigor by encouraging deeper synthesis, gap prioritization, and improved reference formatting.

Refined Prompt for Grok 3

Addresses descriptive tendencies by emphasizing synthesis, theoretical grounding, and balanced PRISMA-style structure.

4. Cross-Model Collaboration

A new synthesis prompt was used to integrate the strongest elements across drafts, prioritizing methodological transparency, cross-domain insights, and real-world deployment challenges.

5. Reflection

This process demonstrated that AI is most effective for structured academic reviews when used iteratively and comparatively. Different models contributed complementary strengths—structure, synthesis, and analytical precision—while scholarly judgment remained essential for coherence, validity, and originality.
