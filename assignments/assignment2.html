Cross-Model Prompting Workflow for an SLR on DM/ML Applications

Structured notes and refined prompts (ChatGPT, Copilot, Grok 3) prepared for GitHub Pages.

1. Initial Prompt 2. Model Analysis 3. Prompt Refinement 4. Collaboration 5. Reflection

1. Initial Prompt Creation Baseline

Baseline Prompt
Goal: a ~2,000-word structured systematic literature review (SLR). 
Conduct a 2,000-word structured systematic literature review on the applications of
data mining and machine learning in real-world domains. Include a methodology
section, synthesize key findings, identify major research trends and gaps, and
propose one testable hypothesis. Use an academic tone and follow systematic
literature review standards.

2. Analyze Model Responses

Three AI models were evaluated across structure, methodological rigor, synthesis quality, domain coverage, research gaps, and hypothesis formulation. Copilot demonstrated the strongest structural rigor, ChatGPT excelled at conceptual synthesis, and Grok 3 provided rich technical detail but weaker systematic integration.

3. Refine the Prompt

Model-specific prompt refinements were introduced to address each system’s limitations while preserving their strengths. Emphasis was placed on PRISMA reporting, quality appraisal, and conservative hypothesis formulation.

4. Cross-Model Collaboration

Insights from all three models were integrated into a single, coherent systematic literature review. Redundant content was removed, terminology standardized, and priority given to deployment-oriented research challenges.

5. Reflection

This process demonstrated that AI is most effective for academic research when used iteratively and comparatively. Structure, synthesis, and analytical precision emerged from different models, reinforcing that scholarly judgment remains essential for coherence, validity, and originality.
